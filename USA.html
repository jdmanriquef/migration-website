how is migration USA

In 2023, the U.S. saw a record number of immigrants at 47.8 million, making up 14.3% of the total population. This was an increase of over 1.6 million from the previous year, the largest annual growth in over 20 years. Immigration is a major driver of population growth in the U.S., with international migration accounting for a significant portion of the nation's population increase. More than 47.8 million immigrants lived in the United States in 2023, the most in U.S. history. That year, immigrants comprised 14.3 percent of the U.S. population of 331.9 million, close to the record level of 14.8 percent set in 1890.

what is the cost of living in usa

The cost of living in the USA varies widely, but generally, it's considered high, especially in larger cities and states like California and New York. A single person might spend around $2,924 per month, while a family of four could expect $7,101. Housing is a significant expense, often taking up a large portion of the budget.
