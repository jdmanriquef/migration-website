FINLAND

Migration in finland

Immigration to Finland comes primarily from Estonia (12.25%), Sweden (8.65%), and Iraq (4.74%). In recent years, the number of immigrants living in Finland has increased by 71,196 people, or 22.61%.

Advantages

Finland is a country with a high standard of living, a good work-life balance, and great job and financial security. It also has a universal healthcare system, a quality state education system, and accessible public transportation.

Disadvantages

The language barrier: Finnish is quite difficult to learn.

Long lines to rent municipal housing.

High taxes.

At first glance, local residents may seem unfriendly.

Finding a job can be difficult given the high competition.
